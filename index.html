THDC Institute of Hydropower Engineering and Technology is a newly established (in Aug 2011) first constituent institute of Uttarakhand Technical University with the aim of becoming a world class comprehensive institute with mission of high-quality undergraduate programs and high caliber research development in cutting-edge areas. The institute is situated at B. Puram, Tehri, approximately 150 km from capital city – Dehradun on beautiful site of THDC project.

More About Us

Vice Chancellor: Prof. P. K. Garg

Prof. P.K. Garg, is currently serving as Vice Chancellor, Uttarakhand Technical University, Dehradun. He completed B.Tech (Civil Engg.) in 1980 and M.Tech (Civil Engg) in 1982 both from the University of Roorkee (now IIT Roorkee). He is a recipient of Gold Medal at IIT Roorkee to stand first during M.Tech programme, Commonwealth Scholarship Award for doing Ph.D. from University of Bristol (UK), and Commonwealth Fellowship Award to carry out post-doctoral research work at University of Reading (UK). He joined the Department of Civil Engg at IIT Roorkee in 1982, and gradually advancing his career rose to the position of Head of the Department in 2015. Dr Garg has shown extra-ordinary capabilities academically and administratively at various positions at IIT Roorkee.

Director: Prof. Geetam Singh Tomar

Prof. Geetam Singh Tomar is a Director of THDC Institute of Hydropower Engineering and Technology, Tehri. He received his UG, PG, and PhD degrees in Electronics Engineering from Institution of Engineers Calcutta, MNREC Allahabad and RGPV Bhopal respectively. He Post-doc from University of Kent, UK in Computer Engineering. He worked with MITS and IIITM Gwalior, University of Kent UK. University of West Indies, Trinidad and Tobago.He also served as Director in 3 private colleges along with additional charge of the Director of Machine Intelligence Research Labs, Gwalior, India. His research interests are Digital Systems, Air Interface and Advanced communication networks.He has published more than 180 research papers and 02 patents.



Our Mission and Vision

THDC-IHET RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT CENTER



Establishment:

It is delighted to inform that THDC Institute of Hydropower Engineering and Technology has initiated THDC-IHET RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT CENTER on Jan 21, 2016 with the objective to promote Renewable Energy Innovation by teams of scientists and engineers from the institute and any other Institutes affiliated to UTU













Areas under the RDC:

  • Solar Energy
  • Wind Power
  • Hydro Power
  • Second Generation Biofuels
  • Network and Database on Renewable Energy











Opportunities:

The center is inviting research proposals from faculty and research scholars with the sole aim to promote R&D activities in new and emerging areas under Solar Energy and Conversion of Bio-waste to Energy. The duration of each project will be 1.5 years and funding will be provided up to 50,000/- to be sanctioned by the institute only after the acceptance and approval by the THDC-IHET Research and Development committee.
